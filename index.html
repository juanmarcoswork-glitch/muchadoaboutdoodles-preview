Goldendoodle Sizes: Mini, Medium & Standard

Mini Goldendoodle

Ideal for smaller homes or families who want a more compact companion. Minis typically weigh 15–35 lbs and carry all the personality of their larger siblings.

Medium Goldendoodle

The sweet spot. A medium — usually 35–50 lbs — is active enough to hike with you and calm enough to curl up on the couch.

Standard Goldendoodle

The classic. Standards (50–90 lbs) thrive in homes with yards and love being the center of family life.

The Allergy-Friendly Choice

Concerned about shedding or allergies? Our Goldendoodles are bred for low-shedding, allergy-friendly coats — among the closest to non-shedding a dog can get. Most allergy sufferers react to dander rather than fur, and a low-shedding coat spreads far less of it around your home.

Whether you're looking for a wavy coat or a tight curl, our puppies are soft, smart, and endlessly snuggly.

Goldendoodle FAQ

Goldendoodles (also called Groodles) combine the intelligence of the Poodle with the gentle temperament of the Golden Retriever. Here's everything families ask us before bringing one home.

Appearance & Coat

How big do Goldendoodles get?

Minis stand 13–15 inches and weigh 15–35 lbs; mediums run 35–50 lbs; standards reach 22+ inches and 50–90 lbs. See our full size comparison guide.

What colors do they come in?

Golden, apricot, black, copper, white, cream, red, chocolate, and gray — a range as broad as their Poodle parent's.

How do Goldendoodles look?

Like a teddy bear, in the best way. Coats range from tight curls to loose, shaggy waves, with plenty of variation between individual dogs.

Do Goldendoodles shed?

Most shed far less than the average dog, though coats taking after the Golden Retriever parent may shed lightly. Curlier coats shed least. Note that most allergy sufferers react to dander rather than fur.

What is the grooming routine?

Brush regularly and visit the groomer every few weeks. Curlier coats need more frequent brushing; most owners keep coats trimmed to prevent matting.

Temperament & Family Life

What is their personality like?

Intelligent and adoring. Goldendoodles are gentle, patient, and grow up affectionate and obedient when trained with positive reinforcement. Read our temperament guide.

Are they good with kids and other pets?

Yes — they're highly sociable and do well with children, other dogs, and even cats. Their trusting, friendly nature does make them poor watchdogs, and early socialization is still essential.

How much space do they need?

They thrive on human contact and are prone to separation anxiety, so they do best living indoors with the family. A house with a fenced yard is ideal; a Mini can adapt to apartment living with enough daily exercise.

Do they like to play?

Constantly. Like their Golden Retriever and Poodle parents, they instigate friendships through play and have the stamina to keep up with active families.

What type of weather do they prefer?

Mild weather. Many enjoy a swim in the heat (never force a reluctant puppy), and they'll want shade in summer and a warm spot to snuggle in winter.

Training & Exercise

How do you train a Goldendoodle puppy?

With time, consistency, and positive reinforcement — they love to please and learn quickly. We offer both standard puppies and fully trained puppies with obedience foundations already in place.

Are they intelligent?

Very — both parent breeds rank among the smartest dogs. The flip side: they pick up bad habits as easily as good ones, so training should be firm and consistent, never harsh.

Are they easy to train and obedient?

Yes, with early training and plenty of stimulation. Their eagerness to please produces highly obedient adults — one reason they're sought after as therapy, service, and guide dogs.

How much exercise do they need?

A minimum of 30 minutes — ideally one hour — of moderate to vigorous activity per day keeps them healthy and calm.

Health & Lifespan

What is their health like?

Generally healthy, though like any breed they have predispositions to watch. We health-test all parent dogs against 164 common conditions and every puppy goes home with a veterinary health certificate.

How long do Goldendoodles live?

Goldendoodles live for between 10 and 15 years.

What is the history of the breed?

Breeders crossed the Golden Retriever and Poodle in the 1990s, following the Labradoodle and Cockapoo. There's no formal breed standard yet, which is why coats and sizes vary. Learn what F1, F1b and F2 generations mean.

Any other questions about Goldendoodles?

Please feel free to contact us with any questions or concerns you may have about our doodles. We are here to help and provide you with all the information you need.
