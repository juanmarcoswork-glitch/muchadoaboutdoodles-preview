The Breed

Meet the Goldendoodle: America's Favorite Designer Dog

A Goldendoodle is a cross between a Golden Retriever and a Poodle — two of the most intelligent, loyal breeds in the world. The result? A dog that is gentle with children, easy to train, low-shedding, and endlessly loving, with that unmistakable teddy-bear charm. Our Virginia-based kennel breeds for health and temperament first.

Coat, shedding & allergies

Bred for low-shedding, allergy-friendly coats — among the closest to non-shedding a dog can get. Coats range from tight curls (shed least, need more grooming) to loose, shaggy waves; most owners keep them trimmed to prevent matting.

Temperament & training

Intelligent, patient, and adoring — highly sociable with children, other dogs, and even cats. They learn fast and respond best to positive, consistent training. Plan on 30–60 minutes of activity a day and plenty of human company.

Questions & Answers

Goldendoodle FAQ

Appearance & Coat

How big do Goldendoodles get?

Minis stand 13–15 inches and weigh 15–35 lbs; mediums run 35–50 lbs; standards reach 22+ inches and 50–90 lbs. See our full size comparison guide.

What colors do they come in?

Golden, apricot, black, copper, white, cream, red, chocolate, and gray — a range as broad as their Poodle parent's.

Do Goldendoodles shed?

Most shed far less than the average dog, though coats taking after the Golden Retriever parent may shed lightly. Curlier coats shed least. Note that most allergy sufferers react to dander rather than fur.

What is the grooming routine?

Brush regularly and visit the groomer every few weeks. Curlier coats need more frequent brushing; most owners keep coats trimmed to prevent matting.

Temperament & Family Life

Are they good with kids and other pets?

Yes — they're highly sociable and do well with children, other dogs, and even cats. Their trusting, friendly nature does make them poor watchdogs, and early socialization is still essential.

How much space do they need?

They thrive on human contact and are prone to separation anxiety, so they do best living indoors with the family. A house with a fenced yard is ideal; a Mini can adapt to apartment living with enough daily exercise.

What type of weather do they prefer?

Mild weather. Many enjoy a swim in the heat (never force a reluctant puppy), and they'll want shade in summer and a warm spot to snuggle in winter.

Training & Exercise

How do you train a Goldendoodle puppy?

With time, consistency, and positive reinforcement — they love to please and learn quickly. We offer both standard puppies and fully trained puppies with obedience foundations already in place.

Are they intelligent and easy to train?

Very — both parent breeds rank among the smartest dogs, and their eagerness to please produces highly obedient adults. The flip side: they pick up bad habits as easily as good ones, so be firm and consistent, never harsh.

How much exercise do they need?

A minimum of 30 minutes — ideally one hour — of moderate to vigorous activity per day keeps them healthy and calm.

Health & Lifespan

What is their health like?

Generally healthy, though like any breed they have predispositions to watch. We health-test all parent dogs against 164 common conditions and every puppy goes home with a veterinary health certificate.

How long do Goldendoodles live?

Goldendoodles live for between 10 and 15 years.

What is the history of the breed?

Breeders crossed the Golden Retriever and Poodle in the 1990s, following the Labradoodle and Cockapoo. There's no formal breed standard yet, which is why coats and sizes vary.

Any other questions about Goldendoodles?

We're here to help with anything about our doodles — from coat types to delivery logistics. Real humans, fast answers.
